Presenter Name · Hype vs Reality05
The technical why

One asymmetry, five mechanisms

AI industrialised generation. It didn't industrialise verification. Everything in this deck is a corollary.
Trained to guess
"I don't know" doesn't score on evals, so models learn confidence (OpenAI, 2025). The eval, not the use case. Vanity metrics, at model scale.
Modal, not median
Models output the most common pattern, not the best one. The mode is old Stack Overflow. Result: 45% insecure, churn up (Veracode, GitClear).
Distribution shift
+30-35% greenfield, ~0% complex brownfield, same tool (Stanford, 100k devs). The famous numbers sample one gradient. Retrieval narrows it: why agentic differs.
Error compounds
Agent success decays exponentially with task length (METR; Ord). Illustration: 99% per step = 1 in 3 over 100 steps. Gates reset the decay.
Little's law
Generation rate +98%; review rate unchanged. Queueing predicts batching, waits, skips. Observed: +154% size, +91% time, 31% unreviewed.
Amdahl sets the ceiling.
Verification sets the moat.
Coding is 11-16% of dev time: 10x it and the org gains ~1.2x (Microsoft, IDC, Atlassian). The durable edge is the verification stack.

THE FACT

THE MYTH
"Klarna's reversal proves the AI savings were fake"
THE FACT
Both halves are true: $60M saved (Q3 2025 earnings call) AND humans rehired for complex tickets.
Company-reported, unaudited. The hybrid model won. And our own checking first false-killed the $60M: challenge the verifier too.
THE MYTH
"Models hallucinate under 1% now"
THE FACT
Best on the May 2026 leaderboard: 1.8%, grounded summarisation only.
Several frontier models run 10 to 23% on the same task; legal queries hit 17 to 88% depending on controls.

The pattern: single-number anchors die most often · numbers mutate as they travel · staleness masquerades as fact

Where it's oversold

AI washing is real, and it's being prosecuted

Builder.ai$500M+raised. "Natasha AI" was ~700 engineers. Bankrupt May 2025.
Presto · SEC95→30%claimed vs actual automation. Humans in Manila took the orders.
Workado · FTC98→53%claimed vs tested accuracy. A coin-flip, sold as AI detection.
"Agentic" vendors~130genuine, of thousands claiming it (Gartner). The rest: agent washing.
The FTC standard: evidence before the claim, not assembled after the challenge. The right rule for advisers too.

So how do you catch it?

Five questions to ask anything "AI-powered"

Use them on vendors, acquisition targets, and your own teams. Each red flag comes from a real enforcement case.

ASK THIS
WALK AWAY WHEN
1 "Walk me through one real request, end to end." They can't, or every path runs through a human (Builder.ai) 2 "What's the human-in-the-loop ratio, in production?" Over ~30%: it's human-assisted software (Presto) 3 "Show me the eval harness, not the headline number." It is. The task and the controls set the rate, not the model.

Why coding leads: the compiler and tests are free, instant fact-checkers. Legal and medical pay a human expert per check.

Presenter Name · Hype vs Reality12
The reality, in lines you can re-use

Seven one-liners, and the so what

  • AI amplifies your organisation; it doesn't multiply on its own.
  • The typing was never the bottleneck.
  • Usage isn't value. 14% of CFOs see impact; 91% increase spend.
  • No baseline, no claim.
  • Buy + integrate + reshape beats build + bolt-on + pilot.
  • Hallucination is a defect rate, not a mystery.
  • Evidence before the claim, for vendors and for us.
THE SO WHAT
None of this says don't invest. It says: pick three workflows, baseline them, reshape end-to-end, and measure honestly. The gains that are real are there to be captured, and most of the market is still measuring nothing.
